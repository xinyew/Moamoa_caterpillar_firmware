20260702 - Caterpillar - Why VDC saturates at 3.1V

Earlier board testing (20260621 - Caterpillar - Board testing and MAX5419 saturation) turned up a puzzling behavior: VDC appeared to saturate at ~3.1 V — any set-point above that made the rail collapse to ~1.04 V — and the MAX5419 digipot was the prime suspect. This note explains the real mechanism (traced through the schematic with Claude Fable 5): it’s a brown-out reset, not digipot saturation.

The two observed numbers map cleanly onto the mechanism:

  • ~3.1 V “saturation” = the value firmware reprograms on every boot (max5419_set_voltage(MOTOR_VDC_V) → tap 230). After any reset the rail lands back at 3.1 V, so it looks like a ceiling.
  • ~1.04 V drop = the MAX5419 NV-EEPROM factory default (midscale, tap 128 ≈ 1.0 V), loaded when the digipot itself power-cycles on a deep brown-out.

A BLE write asking for VDC ≥ ~3.7 V is what trips the reset. 3.7 V isn’t a random number — it’s the 1S-LiPo terminal voltage under load, i.e. where the STBB1 boost converter’s input current runs away.

Power tree

Shared-battery power tree

Both converters share one raw battery node via the M3 header. The MCU only resets if the 3.3 V rail dies, which only dies if +BATT itself collapses. That coupling through the shared battery node is the whole story.

Why exactly ~3.7 V

  1. Below ~3.7 V VDC: STBB1 is in buck mode. Input current < output current (~85–95 %). Battery sees a modest, well-behaved load — 3.3 V works flawlessly.
  2. At VDC ≈ V_batt: enters the buck-boost transition region; above it, boost mode. Two things happen at once:
    • Input current now exceeds output current: I_in ≈ I_out × V_out/(V_batt × η). Motor load also grows as V² — at 3.7 V it draws ~40 % more power than at 3.1 V.
    • The transition region (V_in ≈ V_out) is where every buck-boost has its worst efficiency and loop stability — and the STBB1 MODE pin is floating (confirmed in netlist), so power-save/PWM behavior in exactly this region is undefined.
  3. +BATT sags. STBB1 input current spikes each PWM on-phase (50 % duty at ~113 Hz — long ~4 ms pulses caps can’t bridge). Support is only ~27 µF total on VDC in 0402 (derates to ~6–8 µF real at 3.7 V DC bias) + battery IR + thin headers.
  4. ADP2503-3.3 follows. Also a buck-boost (needs V_in ≥ ~2.3 V, finite caps). When +BATT dips deep enough, 3.3 V drops below the nRF54L15 brown-out threshold → POR. Reboot reprograms the digipot to 3.1 V, STBB1 falls back to buck, everything recovers.

Why the firmware ramp fix didn’t help: 3.6 V vs 3.7 V is byte-identical bar one digipot tap. Ramping spreads the transition out, but boost-mode steady-state demand is what kills it — it dies roughly one tap after VDC crosses battery voltage.

Follow-up: MAX5419 reset behavior on brown-out (Fable)

Confirms where the 3.1 V recovery value actually comes from — it is not a digipot default.

The MAX5419 has no reset pin and doesn’t know the nRF54 rebooted. It only resets when its own supply (the 3.3 V rail) power-cycles below its POR threshold; on POR it loads the wiper from internal NV EEPROM, which still holds the factory default (midscale, tap 128 ≈ 1.0 V) because firmware never writes the NV register (CMD_V2NV is defined in max5419.c but never used).

Two digipot outcomes on a brown-out

  1. Shallow dip (3.3 V sags to ~1.7 V, MCU resets, digipot rides it out): wiper keeps its volatile value (e.g. tap 235 from mid-ramp). The digipot’s min operating voltage is 2.7 V, so in the 1.7–2.7 V gray zone the volatile state is undefined — but it does not go to 3.1 V by itself.
  2. Deep dip (digipot PORs too): wiper reloads NV → tap 128 → rail ≈ 1.0 V. This is the ~1.04 V seen in testing.

Neither path produces 3.1 V. The 3.1 V comes from main.c: every boot, max5419_set_voltage(MOTOR_VDC_V) → tap 230 (RTT: “Target 3.10 V -> tap 230” within ~15 ms of the banner).

Sequence during a reset event: ramp reaches ~3.7 V → +BATT collapses → MCU brown-out → (digipot holds ~tap 235, or PORs to 128 → ~1.04 V) → firmware boots and overwrites the wiper to tap 230 → VDC = 3.1 V.

Implications:

  • The boot-order fix (digipot before STBB1 EN) is critical — a stale/default tap never reaches an enabled rail.
  • During the reset itself P2.03 is Hi-Z and STBB1 EN floats (no board pulldown); with unlucky leakage the converter could enable while the digipot still holds the pre-crash tap ~235 → reinforces the 100 k EN pulldown fix.
  • Programming the digipot NV EEPROM to 3.1 V (one-shot CMD_V2NV, ~50 k write endurance, via a BLE command or build flag) would make the power-up default 3.1 V instead of 1.0 V — defense in depth for that window.

Falsifiable predictions (not yet tested)

  1. Fully charge the battery to 4.2 V → the reset point should shift up above ~4 V; 3.5 V and even 3.7 V requests should then start failing.
  2. Scope +BATT and the 3.3 V rail while stepping 3.5 → 3.8 V → synchronized dips at the PWM rate on +BATT, then 3.3 V follows.
  3. Bodge STBB1 MODE to 3.3 V (forced PWM) → should visibly raise the threshold (fixed-frequency, max dynamic performance per the ST datasheet), though it can’t fix the arithmetic of boost mode.
