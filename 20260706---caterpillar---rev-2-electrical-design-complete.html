Key design decisions

  • Power isolation: parallel buck-boost architecture, per-converter input caps, stiff LiPo — the 3.3 V and 0–4.5 V rails won’t interfere; ground layout is the remaining variable.
  • Bug caught: MOSI double-connected / MISO floating in the first SPI draft.
  • Legacy revision list (8 items): EN pulldown ✔, MODE grounded ✔, reverse polarity via keyed JST ✔, digipot ceiling via R9 ✔; bulk caps + short leads obsoleted by the 30C pack; battery divider + status LED deliberately dropped (conditions recorded).
  • C7 removed: eval-board precedent + R9’s HF flooring of the FB leg; final verdict deferred to first-article scope test.
  • MLCC DC-bias: C9 (VDC 22 µF) upgraded to 10 V-rated 0603 to keep ≥ 10 µF effective at 4.63 V.
  • 4-layer decision: the +4 SPI nets on a congested board tipped it (I²C stays for the digipot).

VDC sensing circuit

VDC telemetry divider added — review confirms the design is correct for its role (quasi-static rail monitoring):

VDC_SENSE divider

  • Ratio: ÷2 → 4.5 V max VDC → 2.25 V at pin, safe inside SAADC range at 3.3 V supply ✓
  • Drain: 200 kΩ = 22.5 µA worst case, negligible vs 400 mA motor ✓
  • Filter: RC ≈ 5 ms (~32 Hz cutoff), good anti-alias; firmware needs ~40 µs SAADC acquisition time (50 kΩ Thevenin impedance).
  • Scope: measures quasi-static VDC after the digipot settles (wait ~5τ ≈ 25 ms after a setpoint change). Cannot see transients > 32 Hz (motor-kick ripple, PFM bursts, settling edges) — deliberate tradeoff, correct for telemetry.
  • Pin: P1.11 must be confirmed AIN-capable on nRF54L15 (AIN region ~P1.04–P1.14; verify against datasheet/devicetree before committing).
  • Escalation ladder if more capability is needed later: (1) 10k/10k divider + 1 nF → ~16 kHz BW; (2) add an op-amp buffer; (3) INA219/226 shunt on the motor path for real power monitoring.
